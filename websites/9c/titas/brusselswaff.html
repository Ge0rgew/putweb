Brussels Waffle

The origin of Brussels waffles is situated around 1850, when the first waffle irons were introduced at the weekly market near the Brussels North railway station. Liège waffles date back even further, as early as the 18th century, when the Prince-Bishop of Liège asked his cook to prepare a tasty and sugary snack.

The Brussels waffle is the lightest variation. It’s fluffy with a crispy touch, tasteful without being overly sweet and comes in a rectangular shape.. Because snowy egg white is the key ingredient to this airy waffle, the batter needs to be made daily fresh.
